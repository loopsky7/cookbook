# Merluza a la Plancha — Restaurant Style

How coastal restaurants in Gran Canaria (and Spanish chefs generally) get hake that's browned, garlicky, glossy, well salted and juicy — from a frozen fillet and a small plancha.

## Ingredients (2 portions)

- 2 thick merluza pieces: skin-on fillets (~250 g each) or rodajas 2–4 cm thick
- Fine sea salt (for brine or last-second salting) + coarse sea salt (optional, for the plancha)
- Sunflower oil for the plancha (fine — it's neutral and heat-stable); olive oil for the finish
- Optional: a little flour for dusting
- Optional finish: 4–6 tbsp EVOO, 2–3 garlic cloves sliced, 1 tsp sweet pimentón, splash of vinegar, parsley

## Step 0 — Buy smart (half the battle)

Most frozen merluza sold in the Canaries is Cape hake, often soaked in phosphates (E451) to pump in water. That water floods your plancha later.

- Ingredients list should say **just "merluza"**. Skip anything with E451 or "con agua añadida".
- Prefer **IQF / ultracongelado** (individually blast-frozen): much less cell damage, much less drip.
- **"Congelado a bordo"** (frozen at sea) often beats tired "fresh" fish.
- Bag full of frost/ice shards = temperature abuse = watery fish. Skip it.

## Step 1 — Thaw right

- **Best**: overnight in the fridge, unwrapped, on paper towels (change them once). Snip vacuum packs open.
- **Fast**: sealed bag in cold water, 20–60 min, refresh water. Cook immediately.
- Never microwave, never the counter.

## Step 2 — The restaurant salt trick (pick one)

- **Option A — brine bath (recommended)**: dissolve **100 g salt in 1 L cold water** (10% brine). Submerge the fish **5–10 min** (thin fillets 5–6, thick rodajas 10). This is how the Torres brothers, Marc Ribas and Joan Roca do it: perfectly even salting, juicier flesh.
- **Option B — dry-brine**: salt both sides (~¾ tsp per 450 g), leave uncovered on a rack in the fridge 4–8 h. Great browning.
- **Option C — last-second**: salt generously **seconds** before it hits the plancha.
- **Never** salt with dry salt 15–45 min ahead: that's the window where osmotic water sits on the surface and ruins the sear.

## Step 3 — Dry obsessively

Press between paper towels until **bone dry**. Change paper, flip, repeat. If skin-on, scrape the skin with the back of a knife, then towel again. Every drop of surface water has to boil off before browning can start — this is the single most important step.

Optional pro trick: whisper-thin **flour dusting**, excess shaken off. Wicks residual moisture and fries into a golden crust.

## Step 4 — Sear

1. Take fish out of the fridge ~20–30 min ahead (tempering).
2. Preheat the **empty** plancha 5+ min on high. Brush on ~1 tbsp oil, wait until it shimmers / first wisp of smoke (~180–200 °C surface).
3. Optional anti-stick: scatter **coarse salt** on the hot plate, or lay a sheet of oiled baking parchment under the fish (both are documented chef tricks).
4. Fish down — skin side first if skin-on. **Press gently 30–60 s** so it doesn't curl.
5. **Hands off 3–4 min.** It sticks at first, then *self-releases* when the crust forms — that's your flip signal. Cue: the top turns from translucent to white.
6. Flip once. 1.5–2 min more (thin) / up to 4 min (4 cm rodajas). Total under ~10 min.
7. Target: center **~55 °C** / just opaque / bone pulls free easily (Arguiñano's test). Pull early — carryover finishes it. Past 60 °C hake turns chalky.

## Step 5 — The garlic finish (this is the "restaurant" part)

The glossy, garlicky surface does NOT come from the grilling. It's a hot garlic-olive-oil poured over at the end. See [`garlic-finishes.md`](garlic-finishes.md) for all four versions; the quick one:

> While the fish rests: 4–6 tbsp EVOO in a small pan + 2–3 sliced garlic cloves. Fry until *just* golden. Off the heat, wait 1 min, stir in 1 tsp sweet pimentón. Pour over the plated fish with chopped parsley.

## Step 6 — Rest & serve

Rest 3–4 min, crust-side up. Serve **immediately** — hake dries out fast. Full Canarian plate: fish topped with ajitos fritos + mojo verde + papas arrugadas.

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Watery, pale, steamed | Wet surface / phosphate-pumped fish / cold plancha | Step 0 + Step 3 + preheat harder |
| Sticks and tears | Moved too early / too little heat / too much oil | Wait for self-release; thin oil film; coarse salt or parchment trick |
| Bland inside | Salted wrong or not at all | 10% brine bath |
| Dry, chalky, crumbly | Overcooked | Pull at ~55 °C; total <10 min |
| Tastes flat | Sunflower-only, no finish | The dish needs the garlic-EVOO pour-over |
