# Garlic Finishes — the "Restaurant" Flavor

The garlicky, glossy, slightly oily surface on Spanish restaurant fish is a **separate sauce poured over at the end**, never part of the grilling itself. Four documented versions, easiest first.

## A) Majado (raw garlic-parsley oil) — the classic

Pound in a mortar to a paste:

- 3 garlic cloves
- Big bunch of parsley
- Pinch of salt
- 4–6 tbsp EVOO

Spoon over the fish **after flipping**, flip once more for a final minute so the garlic barely cooks (raw garlic on hot fish = harsh; a minute of heat = sweet).
Blender variant ("salsa Mery"): EVOO + garlic + lemon juice + parsley, blended; brush on after 3 min skin-side, flip, more sauce, 2 min.
Trick: 10 s in the microwave for the unpeeled clove mellows it (Recetas de Escándalo).

## B) Ajada (Galician fried garlic + pimentón oil)

1. Generous EVOO (4–6 tbsp) in a small pan, **2–3 garlic cloves sliced thin**, fry until just golden.
2. **Remove from heat**, wait 1–2 min (so the pimentón doesn't burn bitter), stir in **1 tbsp sweet pimentón**.
3. Rest a moment so the pimentón sediments → clean red garlic oil. Drizzle over the fish.
4. Optional: splash of vinegar or fish stock.

## C) Bilbaína refrito (Basque pour-over)

- Good glug of EVOO + **4–5 sliced garlic cloves**, cook until lightly golden.
- Add a **cayenne/guindilla**, 1 min.
- **Splash of vinegar** (careful, it spits), cook 2 min to bind.
- Pour the whole hot sauce over the plancha-cooked merluza, shower with chopped parsley.

This is the closest documented restaurant recipe to "grilled hake with garlic oil" — it's literally merluza a la plancha + garlic refrito.

## D) Canarian style: ajitos fritos + mojo verde

Per Gran Canaria food writer Laube Leal: fish "a la espalda" or a la plancha is **"most normally served simply with ajitos fritos on top"** — garlic slices crisped in olive oil and poured over — or with **mojo verde**.

**Ajitos fritos**: 6–7 sliced garlic cloves started in *cold* EVOO with 1–2 guindillas, brought up to golden-toasty, poured hot over the fish with a squeeze of lemon.

**Mojo verde de cilantro** (blender):
- Big bunch fresh cilantro (or parsley)
- 1 garlic clove
- 1 tsp cumin
- Pinch of salt
- Splash of water + 2–3 tbsp vinegar
- Drizzle in ~150 ml olive oil while blending to emulsify
- Optional: a few raw almonds for a creamier texture (Laube Leal's variant)

**Full Canarian plate**: fish + ajitos fritos or mojo verde + **papas arrugadas** (small potatoes boiled in very salty water until the skins wrinkle and crust with salt).
