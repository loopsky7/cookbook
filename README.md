# Cookbook

Personal cooking knowledge base. Focus right now: **high-protein, delicious food** — starting with grilled fish, Canarian-restaurant style.

## The problem this repo solves first

Frozen merluza (hake) at home on the plancha = pale, watery, steamed. In Gran Canaria restaurants = browned, garlicky, glossy with oil, properly salted, juicy. This repo documents exactly how they do it, with the food science behind it.

## TL;DR — the 5 fixes

1. **Buy better frozen fish**: IQF, ingredient list says only "merluza" (no E451/phosphates, no "con agua añadida"), minimal ice glaze, no frost in the bag.
2. **Thaw + dry obsessively**: overnight in the fridge on paper towels; press dry until bone-dry. Water on the surface = steaming, not searing.
3. **Restaurant salt trick**: 10 min soak in 10% brine (100 g salt per 1 L cold water), then dry again completely. Even seasoning + juicier flesh. (Or salt only seconds before cooking — never 15–45 min ahead with dry salt.)
4. **Screaming-hot plancha, don't touch the fish**: preheat 5+ min, thin film of oil until it just smokes, fish down, press 30 s, then hands off 3–4 min until it self-releases and is golden. Flip once, 2 min more. Center ~55 °C.
5. **The garlicky, glossy finish is a separate step**: olive oil + sliced garlic fried until golden (optionally pimentón off-heat, or a splash of vinegar bilbaína-style) poured over the plated fish. That IS the restaurant surface.
